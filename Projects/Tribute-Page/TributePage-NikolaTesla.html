Nikola Tesla

Man out of time

Here's a time line of Tesla's life:

  • 1856 - Born in Smiljan, Croatia
  • 1875 - Tesla went to Austria to study at the Technical College in Graz.
  • 1882 - He went to work in Paris for the Continental Edison Company
  • 1883 - He constructed, after work hours, his first induction motor.
  • 1884 - He went to the United States to work for Thomas Edison.
  • 1888 - George Westinghouse, head of the Westinghouse Electric Company in Pittsburgh, bought the patent rights to Tesla’s polyphase system of alternating-current dynamos, transformers, and motors.
  • 1889 - Tesla soon established his own laboratory, where his inventive mind could be given free rein.
  • 1891 - He invented Tesla coil, which is widely used today in radio and television sets and other electronic equipment.
  • 1891 - He got U.S. citizenship.
  • 1893 - Westinghouse used Tesla’s alternating current system to light the World’s Columbian Exposition at Chicago. This success was a factor in their winning the contract to install the first power machinery at Niagara Falls, which bore Tesla’s name and patent numbers.
  • 1898 - Tesla announced his invention of a teleautomatic boat guided by remote control. When skepticism was voiced, Tesla proved his claims for it before a crowd in Madison Square Garden.
  • 1900 - Tesla made what he regarded as his most important discovery—terrestrial stationary waves. By this discovery he proved that Earth could be used as a conductor and made to resonate at a certain electrical frequency.
  • 1917 - Tesla was the recipient of the Edison Medal, the highest honour that the American Institute of Electrical Engineers could bestow.
  • 1943 - dies in New York at the age of 86.
